  <h3>Attach Power Supply to Chassis</h3>

  <img src="P8200936Editcopy.jpg">

  <p>Using the M4 button head cap screws, attach the power supply through the mounting holes on the underside of the chassis.

  <h3>Attach Stops</h3>

  <img src="P8200944Editcopy.jpg">

  <p>Install the M4 button head cap screws in the threaded holes on the side of the chassis.  These screws locate the back of the controller board and make the face of the board flush with the chassis.</p>

  <h3>Wire Controller Board</h3>

  <img src="P8200993Editcopy.jpg">

  <p>Attach the cables to the PCB Interface board.  The cables can only be connected in one orientation, so if they don't attach well double check them and try again.  Try to orient them so that the wires can easily travel from board to board.  Make sure to tuck the e-stop cable along the edge of the enclosed power supply to prevent any sort of snagging or pinching.</p>

  <h2 id="install-front-panel">*Install Front Panel*</h2>

  <img src="P8201089Editcopy.jpg">

  <h3>Dissasemble E-Stop Button</h3>

  <img src="P8201008Editcopy.jpg">
  
  <p>Remove the button from the screw terminals by gently pulling on the yellow tab. With the button upside down (as pictured) pull the tab towards you and then to the right to disengage the two parts.  Unscrew the nut attached to the base of the button. This nut will be used to secure the button to the enclosure.</p>

  <h3>Attach E-Stop Button</h3>

  <img src="P8201019Editcopy.jpg">

  <p>Insert the button through the hole on the top panel and secure using the nut.  Make sure the side of the button marked "TOP" is facing the middle of the top panel.  Slide the top panel into the channels of the chassis, far enough back so you can access the Mini USB port on the main controller board.</p>

    <h3>Wire E-Stop And Attach Front Panel</h3>

  <img src="P8201081Editcopy.jpg">

  <p>Attach the ends of the e-stop cable to each side of the NC screw terminal as pictured. Tighten the phillips head screws on the screw terminals to hold the cable in place.  To attach the wired screw terminal to the button, *line up the "TOP" marking on the button with the "TOP" marking on the screw terminal.*  Once in postion, move the yellow tab towards the back of the assembly to lock in place.  Attach the front panel using the included M4 socket head cap screws.</p>
